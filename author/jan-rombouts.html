Hello, and welcome to my website! I am a researcher, currently working on the interface between mathematics, physics and biology. I studied mathematics and complex systems science. During my PhD I looked at the modeling of cell cycle oscillations. Currently I am a postdoctoral fellow at the Université Libre de Bruxelles, where I wonder about how cell-cell interactions lead to patterns and waves.

New preprint on pattern formation

The first preprint from my postdoctoral work, in collaboration with Michael Zhao, Anna Erzberger and Alexander Aulehla is now out! In it, we use nonlocal advection-diffusion models to study aggregation-based patterning. In particular, we ask how the size of a confining domain determines patterning dynamics. We find that whereas the long-time attracting state is always the same, the transient dynamics are highly variable dependent on the domain size. We also did some fun mathematics and predict the outcome of an exciting set of experiments by Michael. Find the preprint on arXiv!

  • 4 February 2025

Preprint: temperature scaling of the embryonic cell cycle

Hot or cold: it matters to us, but also to the dividing egg cell. Over a wide range of temperatures, the hotter it is, the faster the cell divisions go. However, too hot, or too cold, and the cycles stop. In this work, we set out to understand how the scaling of cell cycle duration with temperature originates in the protein interaction network that drives the cycle forward. This was a collaboration with the labs of Jim Ferrell and Qiong Yang. Find the preprint on biorxiv!

  • 15 December 2023

Our model is one of the winners of 'Model of the Year 2023'!

Some years ago during my PhD work, we came up with a new method of modeling bistable switches, based on a modification of the Hill function. We used it to model the transitions between different phases of the cell cycle, described in our 2021 paper. Now, these models have been chosen as one of the three winners of the competition 'Model of the Year 2023'. This competition is held by the BioModels website, and aims to promote reproducibility and good modeling practice in systems biology. I'm happy to be among the winners! Thanks to Jolan and Lendert for working together on the paper. Also check out this year's competition: Model of the Year 2024.

  • 2 November 2023

Review paper on mechanochemical pattern formation published in EMBO Reports

Together with Jenna and Anna, we wrote a review paper on mechanochemical pattern formation. We illustrate using a range of examples how mechanical processes such as flows and forces can be important in the formation of biological patterns. Thanks to Jenna and Anna, and also to the many people who gave feedback on the manuscript!
